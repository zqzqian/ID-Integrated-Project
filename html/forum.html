An increase in GST means that we will need to allocate more of our budget towards essential items, reducing our purchasing power for non-essential goods. Lower-income households may feel the burden disproportionately as a higher percentage of their income goes towards consumption. Businesses may also pass on the increased tax burden to consumers through higher prices. So, an increase in GST leads to reduced spending, impacting both individual budgets and overall economic activity. Of course bad lah!
